About Me

I am a PhD candidate in Astrophysics at HUJI, focusing on high energy astrophysical phenomena. My research interests include collisions in dense stellar systems and self-similar solutions in hydrodynamics.

Beyond research, I am committed to teaching and enjoy a good fantasy book.

Research

Stellar Collisions in Dense Systems

Investigating the collision rates of stars in dense stellar environments such as globular clusters and galactic nuclei, with focus on the limitations of the n∑v approximation in resonant potentials.

Self-Similar Hydrodynamics

Finding self-similar flow solutions for strong shocks, both one-dimensional and two-dimensional.

Computational Astrophysics

Developing numerical methods and simulations to model high-energy astrophysical phenomena, utilizing both custom code and established computational frameworks.

Publications

Revisiting the strong shock problem: Converging and diverging shocks in different geometries

Elisha Modelevsky, Re'em Sari

Physics of Fluids, 2021

An analysis of one-dimensional self-similar flows including a strong shock in various geometries. Establishes a connection between the different geometries and develops many analytical approximations.

Read Paper

The unreasonable effectiveness of the \(n \Sigma v\) approximation

Elisha Modelevsky, Nicholas C. Stone, Re'em Sari

Submitted to ApJ, 2025

We show that the commonly used \(n \Sigma v\) formula is not always valid for stellar collision rates in dense environments, due to the resonant nature of the Kepler and the Harmonic potentials.
